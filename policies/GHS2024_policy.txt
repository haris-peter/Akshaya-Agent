Global Health Scheme (GHS2024) - Official Policy Guidelines
===========================================================

1. Objective
The Global Health Scheme (GHS2024) aims to provide comprehensive health coverage
to economically underprivileged citizens across all districts.

2. Eligibility Criteria
- Citizens must have an annual household income strictly less than INR 5,00,000.
- Applicants must be Indian residents with a valid Aadhar Card.
- There is no district restriction; all districts are covered.

3. Required Documentation
The following documents are mandatory for a successful application:
  a) Aadhar Card - Required for photo identity and address proof.
  b) Income Certificate - Issued by the local Tahsildar or Revenue Officer,
     certifying the household annual income. Must be from the current financial year.
  c) Medical Certificate - Issued by a registered government hospital doctor,
     describing the applicant's current health condition and any pre-existing conditions.

4. Income Certificate Exemptions
Senior citizens above the age of 60 are exempt from submitting the income certificate.

5. Rejection Clauses
  - Applications missing the Aadhar Card will be rejected as identity cannot be verified.
  - Applications missing the medical certificate will be summarily rejected to prevent fraud.
  - Applications with a declared household income exceeding INR 5,00,000 will be rejected.

6. Appeal Process
Rejected applicants may file an appeal at the nearest District Collectorate within 30 days
of receiving the rejection notice.
